One cool article I read was about what skills AI can’t replace. It listed five skills: curiosity, creativity, courage, compassion, and communication. These are fundamental to making new progress, making decisions, and being part of a good team. I think that if we focus on these good things that humans do, we can change how we view AI. But we need to make these a valuable skill in the workplace.

Another article I read was how many customers are unsatisfied with AI chat bots, because they aren’t answering the questions right, and will default to deflection often before actually getting a resolution. The article sort of suggested that there are still a lot of people out there who want the benefits of talking to a real human with real emotions. It also talked about the idea that the AI agent is being told to optimize for the business profits (or similar), and not being encouraged to be as helpful as possible.

Another article I saw was that Wikipedia has decided that you can’t use AI to edit articles. I think this is an important shift that shows we’re not ready for AI yet, it can’t do everything. It shows that we need to start being a little more careful as we use it, because we can’t always trust it.

One of the articles I read was about how AI had been used in the bombing of the girl’s primary school in Iran. By handing over decision making and thinking to AI, the US made a horrible choice simply because it assumed the AI was right, and didn’t bother to go back in a think for themselves. I think this is one of the new biggest issues, not that AI will be able to make it’s own decisions, but that humans will lose the ability to make those decisions for themselves.
