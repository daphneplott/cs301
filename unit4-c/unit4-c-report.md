One article I read pointed out the idea that AI art is maybe only 'good enough'. It is good enough for companies to be able to save money by firing their artists, but it can't ever capture the entire emotion of humanity. However, one thing that worries me is that humans don't really care about that anymore. I think you're going to run into issues because a lot of the use of art now a days is about the transaction, needing art to fulfill a purpose. It's not about having something meaningful, but getting something done.

One thing that I think needs to happen now rather than later is legislature about copyright, ai use disclosure, and similar things. I think that if ai art couldn't be protected under copyright, I think this would help artists because companies don't want to lose their copyright priveleges to certain pieces of work. I think that if ai use was more disclosed, I think that in some areas people will decide they don't want something made by ai. I think that soon ai art will be considered cheap, and there will be a lot of people not interested in that. I wonder though, if there is a place for cheap art. Maybe we can use ai in such a way that it reflects cars, sort of. Where you can still have a car for cheap, but there is also demand for cars that are expensive.

Another article I read talked about the importance of compensating artists for training data. I think this should absolutely fall under copyright protections, because the art is being used to produce a model that is leveraged for profit. I wonder if this is something that computer scientists are blind to because of how much we share code using online platforms like github. It definetly needs to change though. Training data must be under consent and must be under compensation or similar agreement.
